Step 1: "If this"

- Sign up for IFTTT if you haven't already.
- Create a new applet here
- Press on "+This" to pick the Trigger Service
- Type in "Webhooks" in the search box.
- Press on “receive a web request".
- Complete the trigger field by assigning a name for the “Event” (a connected device), Enter something that describes your action. Let's use desk_light for our example.
- Click "Create Trigger".

Step 2: "Then that"

Now we are going to choose action to our service.
- Click on "+That"
- Since our lamp is connected to our TP-Link smart plug, we want ”TP-Link Kasa” to be our service.
- Since our task is to turn on and off our lights, we click on “Toggle”, to be our choice of action for our service.
- We complete the action field by linking our “kasa” device, in this case, my lamp, and by pressing on the “Create action”.
You have now completed the steps to create an IFFFT webhook command for your Lazy App.

Step 3 : Grab your Webhook URL

- Go on this page
- Click on “documentation” on the top righthand corner.
- This will take you to the page to get the URL for your webhook.
- Type in your “event name” in the box. I typed in desk_light because that was the name of the event I created.
- Select the entire URL, copy it to your clipboard.

Step 4 : Add the webhook to Lazy

Now that you have your webhook URL, you can add it to Lazy in order to create your keyboard shortcut to use for future tasks.
- Open the Lazy settings.
- Click on the "+" button to add your new action.
- Assign an emoji and the name you want for this action.
- Paste the webhook URL in the URL box section.
- Assign a keyboard shortcut to the action.

Step 5 : Use your Lazy Shortcut

You are now ready to use your new Lazy keyboard shortcut generated by an IFFFT webhook.
- Simply hit your shortcut on your keyboard. And voila! You’ve just fired your new Lazy command to your connected device
- Press ⌥ + ⌘ + K if you forget a shortcut to launch an action by just typing it's name and press enter.

Going Further...

Use the same webhook URL for other actions, instead of desk_light, just change the event name depending on the new applets that you create
