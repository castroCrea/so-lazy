Senior Full Stack Engineer

Can you remember the first time you felt the superpower of using a CLI?
We want to bring this delightful experience - that engineers enjoy daily - to everyone.
CLIs can be intimidating and feel like a black box for non-technical people and thus were never really adopted widely because of their learning curve.
But at Lazy we strongly believe that we can reunify the experience of a CLI with a classical GUI to build what we call a CLUI: an intuitive interface with a small learning curve that blends GUIs and CLIs to perform actions in the quickest and most intuitive way possible.
Today CLIs are back, but hidden under the name of search and we think it's the perfect time to lend cachet to all the efficiency of the original CLIs.
With that in mind, we launched the first version of Lazy and became the IoT Product of the Year 2019 on Product Hunt.
We're now applying that same UX paradigm to the world of ideas, thoughts and tasks: the next level of Lazy is a smart CLUI that captures a thought with its context at the speed of a keyboard shortcut.
Often when you are in the middle of something, a thought comes to mind that you need to write down, whether it's a lightbulb moment or a reminder. With Lazy, you can write down that thought with a UX that reduces the cognitive cost of jotting something down, respecting your precious flow, and always designed for speed because the most valuable thing in the world is time.
We believe that CLUI is the perfect tool to capture anything from anywhere but unfortunately that won’t help you to be a better thinker or get more done unless there is a good system behind to process those thoughts. That's why we're also building what we call a *Universal Inbox* to collect all your captures.

These different workflows make us rethink productivity from the first principle and it brings a wide range of technical challenges. We need remarkable engineering to pull it off. As a member of our small team, you’ll have a massive impact and plenty of opportunities to both leverage your existing strengths and learn new skills.

What You'll Do

- You'll first build the core features of our MVP closely with our CTO from start to finish — by breaking down high-level goals into tasks and timelines. - You'll then take ownership of the features that you develop. Think features to improve the format of captures that we support, such as a tweet storm captured in a neat way into your *universal inbox*. - You'll build improvements to performance, reliability and product polish — making the Lazy infrastructure scalable and monitor end-user experience. E.g. making every interaction faster than 100ms because it is the threshold where interactions feel instantaneous. - You'll take the lead on important subjects such as the text editor stream. - You'll be in the mindset of mentoring, coaching and supporting a future engineering team from helping in the interview process and recruiting talented engineers. - Technologies we use: React, Redux, Electron, REST API, Docker, Heroku - Technologies we might consider using: Golang, GraphQL, `?`
